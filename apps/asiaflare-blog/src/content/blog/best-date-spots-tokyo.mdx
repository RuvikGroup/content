Tokyo has no shortage of romantic spots, but finding the ones that *feel* right takes local knowledge. We asked our community to share their favourite date locations — here's what they told us.

## Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

One of the most underrated date venues in Tokyo. The garden covers 58 hectares and feels worlds away from the bustle of Shinjuku just outside its gates.

  src="https://www.openstreetmap.org/export/embed.html?bbox=139.7045%2C35.6831%2C139.7163%2C35.6889&layer=mapnik"

<Callout type="tip" title="Best Time to Visit">
  Cherry blossom season (late March – early April) is spectacular but crowded. Autumn (mid-November) offers stunning foliage with far fewer tourists. Weekday mornings are nearly empty year-round.
</Callout>

## Yanaka — Old Tokyo Vibes

If your match appreciates history and quieter streets, Yanaka is a gem. This neighbourhood largely survived WWII and retains Edo-period architecture.

<Callout type="info">
  Yanaka Cemetery sounds unusual for a date spot, but the tree-lined paths and cats that wander freely make it surprisingly romantic — especially at dusk.
</Callout>
