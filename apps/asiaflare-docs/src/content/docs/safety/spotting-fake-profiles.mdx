The vast majority of people on AsiaFlare are genuine, but it's worth knowing the warning signs of fake profiles and scammers so you can protect yourself.

## Warning signs in a profile

- **Too good to be true photos** — very high-quality, model-like images often belong to someone else. Try a reverse image search to check.
- **Very sparse profile** — no bio, only one photo, vague or generic information
- **Inconsistencies** — age, location, or details that don't add up between photos or across the conversation
- **Newly created profile** — while everyone starts somewhere, a brand new profile combined with other red flags is worth noting

## Warning signs in conversation

- **Moving too fast emotionally** — saying "I love you" or making serious relationship claims very quickly, before you've even met
- **Avoiding video calls** — always having an excuse not to video call, even after weeks of messaging
- **Inconsistent details** — their story changes or doesn't match up with earlier messages
- **Redirecting to another platform** — asking you to move to WhatsApp, Telegram, or another app early in the conversation, especially before meeting
- **Asking for money** — this is the clearest sign of a scam. No matter how convincing the story — an emergency, a plane ticket, an investment opportunity — do not send money to someone you have not met in person.

<Aside type="caution">
Scammers are often very patient and convincing. They may spend weeks building a connection before asking for anything. The emotional investment you feel is real — but it doesn't mean the person is.
</Aside>

## What to do

If you suspect a profile is fake or a scam:

1. **Stop engaging** — you don't need to explain yourself
2. **Report the profile** — use the in-app report feature so our moderation team can investigate
3. **Block the user** — this prevents any further contact
4. **Do not send money** — even if you feel you already have a connection
