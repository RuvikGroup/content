Your profile photos are the first thing people see. A strong set of photos can make a significant difference in how many matches and messages you receive.

<img src="/images/guide-profile-photos-hero.png" alt="Example of a strong profile photo set" />

## Use a clear, recent main photo

Your first photo is the most important. It should:

- Show your face clearly — no sunglasses, hats, or heavy filters
- Be well-lit, ideally in natural daylight
- Be recent — within the last year or two
- Feature just you, not a group

<Aside type="tip">
Smiling in your main photo makes you appear more approachable and significantly increases the chance someone will tap on your profile.
</Aside>

## Add variety across your photos

Use your remaining photos to show different sides of yourself:

| Photo type | Why it works |
|------------|--------------|
| Full body photo | Gives a complete picture and builds trust |
| Doing something you enjoy | Shows personality and gives people something to talk about |
| Travelling or outdoors | Suggests an active, interesting lifestyle |
| Social setting with friends | Shows you have a life outside the app |

<img src="/images/guide-profile-photos-variety.png" alt="Example of varied photo types in a profile" />

## What to avoid

- **Group photos as your main photo** — people shouldn't have to guess which one you are
- **Old photos** — meeting someone who looks very different to their photos damages trust immediately
- **Heavy filters or face-altering effects** — they look unnatural and hide what you actually look like
- **Low resolution or blurry images** — they appear unprofessional and are easy to scroll past
- **Shirtless photos in indoor / non-beach settings** — context matters

## How many photos should you have?

Profiles with **4–6 photos** consistently perform better than profiles with just one or two. Each additional photo gives someone another reason to swipe right.

<Aside type="tip">
Ask a friend to take a few photos of you in good lighting — candid shots almost always look more natural than selfies.
</Aside>
