Meeting someone from a dating app for the first time is exciting. A few simple precautions can make sure it stays that way.

<img src="/images/guide-staying-safe-hero.png" alt="Safe first meeting tips" />

## Before you meet

**Video call first** — a quick video call before meeting in person helps confirm the person is who they say they are. It also takes the edge off any first-meeting nerves.

**Tell someone where you're going** — let a friend or family member know who you're meeting, where, and roughly when you'll be back. Share your match's profile with them if you can.

**Do a quick search** — a name and a photo search takes two minutes and can tell you a lot.

## Choose the right location

For a first date, always meet somewhere:

- **Public** — a café, restaurant, or bar with other people around
- **Familiar to you** — somewhere you know and feel comfortable
- **Easy to leave** — avoid situations where you're dependent on the other person for transport

Avoid going to someone's home, or having them come to yours, until you've met a few times and built real trust.

## Getting there and back

- **Arrange your own transport** — drive yourself, use a taxi or ride-share, or take public transport. Don't accept a lift from someone you've just met.
- **Keep your location to yourself** — there's no need to share your home address or exact neighbourhood early on.

## During the date

Trust your instincts. If something feels off, it's okay to leave. You don't owe anyone an explanation.

A simple exit: "I have an early start tomorrow, I'm going to head off" works in any situation.

<Aside type="caution">
Never leave your drink unattended, and be cautious about accepting drinks you didn't see poured.
</Aside>

## After the date

Check in with the friend you told — let them know you're home safe. If anything made you uncomfortable, consider reporting the profile in the app so our team can review it.
